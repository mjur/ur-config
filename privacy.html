Ur — Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 15, 2026

Ur ("the app") is a board game for Android with optional online multiplayer. This policy describes what data the app handles and why. Short version: no accounts, no data sale, and the app itself collects nothing that identifies you personally; advertising and crash reporting are provided by Google and described below.

Data the app collects

  • Display name — a nickname you choose. Shown to your online opponent. Not linked to your real identity.
  • Anonymous player ID — a random identifier generated on your device the first time you play. Used only so the matchmaking server can tell connections apart. It contains no personal information, and it is not backed up: reinstalling the app or moving to a new device creates a new one.
  • Crash diagnostics — if the app crashes, technical information (device model, OS version, stack trace) is reported through Google Firebase Crashlytics so bugs can be fixed. See Firebase privacy documentation.

How online play works

When you play online, your display name, anonymous player ID, and your game moves are sent over an encrypted connection (TLS) to a relay server. The server holds this data in memory only, for the duration of your session — it stores nothing on disk and keeps no history of games or players.

Advertising

The app may show advertising served by Google AdMob. When ads are shown, Google may process your device's advertising ID and ad-interaction data to serve and measure ads; in the European Economic Area and the UK you will be asked for consent before any ad is requested, and you can decline. See how Google uses advertising data. You can reset or delete your advertising ID in your device's Google settings.

What the app does not do

  • No user accounts, email addresses, or passwords.
  • No sale of data. Data is shared only with Google (crash reporting and ad serving) as described above; ad personalization is subject to the consent choices described in the Advertising section.
  • No location, contacts, camera, microphone, or other sensitive device access.

Data deletion

All app data lives on your device. Uninstalling the app (or clearing its storage) deletes your display name and player ID permanently; the server has nothing to delete.

Children

The app is intended for a general audience and is not directed at children. It requires no personal information; nicknames are filtered for offensive content. Any ads shown are served by Google AdMob as described in the Advertising section.
